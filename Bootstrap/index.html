All Around Us

Mushrooms come in several thousand shapes and sizes, and can grow almost anywhere. They thrive in damp, dark conditions and live mostly on decaying plant matter on forest floors. However, some mushrooms can grow in part to full sun, such as those you may find growing on manure in a field. Mushrooms are more likely to adapt to less ideal conditions if they have a quality substrate to grow on.

Everywhere

There are over 6 million species of fungi on the planet! However, only around 14,000 produce a mushroom. Mushrooms are important in most earthbound ecosystems as they provide life-sustaining mineral nutrients to plants while decomposing their remains, and recycling both organic and inorganic byproducts throughout the biome as they grow and reproduce.

Did you know?

Mushrooms are closer to humans than any other plant, according the their DNA. Mushrooms can absorb vitamin D from exposed UV light sources, just like humans!
